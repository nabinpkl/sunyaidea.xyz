Have you ever had an idea and wondered if you were the first to think of it?

Have you ever shared it with someone and then, quietly, worried they might move on it before you?

Did you ever want a way to just lock in "I had this thought, at this moment," without having to trust anyone to enforce it?

I built Sunya Ideas for that exact anxiety.

You type the idea. Your browser hashes it locally. Your wallet signs the hash. The signature lands on a public blockchain under your key, at a block timestamp that no party can forge or backdate.

If priority is ever contested, you reveal the original text. Anyone, without trusting me, can rehash it and confirm you committed exactly this, on exactly this date, under your key.

No Sunya server. No database. No account. The plaintext never leaves your device. I operate a static website and a stateless smart contract, nothing else. If my domain vanished tomorrow, your evidence would survive.

Now the honest part.

To pull this off, just to save one idea, a normal user has to:

- Install a crypto wallet like MetaMask or Rabby, and learn what a seed phrase is
- Open a Coinbase account and clear KYC
- Buy some ETH
- Move it from Coinbase to their wallet
- Keep enough on hand to pay gas
- Tell the difference between Sepolia and Base, between a real signature prompt and a phishing one
- Not panic the first time the gas spikes or the network congests

The underlying primitive is beautiful. The onboarding, today, is brutal.

Closing that gap is the next thing I'm building.
